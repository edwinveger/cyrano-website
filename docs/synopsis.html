The Story

A tale of love, honor, and poetic passion

Overview

Set in 17th century France, our musical adaptation of Edmond Rostand's masterpiece follows the story of Cyrano de Bergerac, a brilliant poet and swordsman with an unusually large nose. Despite his wit and charm, Cyrano believes his appearance makes him unworthy of love.

When Cyrano's cousin, the beautiful Roxane, falls in love with the handsome but inarticulate Christian, Cyrano agrees to help him win her heart by writing love letters on his behalf. Through these letters, Roxane falls in love with Christian's soul, believing it to be his own words.

Key Scenes

Act I: The Theater

Cyrano defends his honor at the theater, displaying both his swordsmanship and poetic wit. Roxane reveals her love for Christian.

Act II: The Letters

Cyrano helps Christian woo Roxane through letters, pouring his own heart into every word while remaining anonymous.

Act III: The Balcony

In one of the most famous scenes, Cyrano speaks to Roxane from the shadows, pretending to be Christian.

Act IV: The Siege

At the siege of Arras, Christian realizes Roxane loves him for Cyrano's words, not his own.

Act V: The Truth

Years later, Roxane discovers the truth about the letters, but it may be too late.

Musical Highlights

  • "The Nose" - Cyrano's opening number
  • "Words of Love" - The letter-writing duet
  • "The Balcony Scene" - The famous romantic ballad
  • "War Song" - The soldiers' chorus at Arras
  • "The Truth at Last" - The final duet
